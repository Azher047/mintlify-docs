Revenue Signals give you a real-time window into the financial performance of private companies. Specter ingests and verifies 500+ revenue disclosures every day from across the web — including off-hand ARR mentions on podcasts, buried figures in press releases, and revenue ranges disclosed in filings — then structures that raw data into clean, comparable signals. Every signal is verified by a combination of AI and human review before it appears in your feed, so you can act on the data with confidence.

## What Revenue Signals Cover

Each revenue signal represents a verifiable financial disclosure tied to a specific company. Signals capture the following data points where available:

- **Revenue or ARR amount** — the disclosed figure in USD
- **Time period** — the period the figure refers to (annual, quarterly, monthly)
- **Revenue type** — ARR, total revenue, or other classification
- **Profitability status** — whether the company disclosed profitability alongside the revenue figure
- **Source** — the original URL where the disclosure was found
- **Confidence score** — Specter's AI-assessed reliability rating for the signal

## Revenue & Valuation Estimates

In addition to event-driven revenue signals, Specter provides continuously modeled **Revenue & Valuation Estimates** for every company in its database. These are live estimates — not point-in-time snapshots — updated monthly and anchored to the verified disclosures captured by Revenue Signals.

Each estimate includes:
- **Modeled monthly revenue** — derived from a combination of verified disclosures, headcount data, and comparable company benchmarks
- **Valuation estimate** — modeled based on revenue multiples, growth signals, and investor activity
- **Confidence bands** — upper and lower bounds that reflect the uncertainty in the underlying data

<Tip>
  Use Revenue & Valuation Estimates when you need a baseline for every company in a list, and Revenue Signals when you want to know the moment a company makes a new financial disclosure.
</Tip>

## API Reference

Use the Revenue Signals endpoint to query verified revenue disclosures programmatically and pipe them into your own models, CRM, or alerts infrastructure.

### Endpoint

```
GET /api/v1/signals/revenue
```

### Query Parameters

| Parameter | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `from_date` | `string` | ISO 8601 date. Returns signals on or after this date. |
| `to_date` | `string` | ISO 8601 date. Returns signals on or before this date. |
| `min_revenue_usd` | `integer` | Filters to signals where the disclosed amount is at or above this value. |
| `max_revenue_usd` | `integer` | Filters to signals where the disclosed amount is at or below this value. |
| `revenue_type` | `string` | `ARR`, `Revenue`, or `Other`. |
| `is_profitable` | `boolean` | When `true`, returns only signals that include a profitability disclosure. |
| `limit` | `integer` | Maximum number of results to return (default: `50`, max: `500`). |

### Example Request

```bash
curl -X GET "https://app.tryspecter.com/api/v1/signals/revenue?from_date=2024-01-01&min_revenue_usd=1000000" \
  -H "X-API-Key: YOUR_API_KEY" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json"
```
