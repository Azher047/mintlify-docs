## Data

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What data points does Specter have on companies?">
    Specter provides 250+ data points per company, spanning:

    - **Firmographics** — industry, sub-sector, founding date, HQ location, employee count, and growth trajectory
    - **Financials** — funding rounds, valuations, revenue estimates, and modeled profitability signals
    - **People** — founders, executives, board members, investors, and key hires with contact details
    - **Signals** — investor interest signals, talent movement signals, revenue signals, and partnership activity
    - **Investors** — associated VC, PE, and angel investors, with round-by-round participation history
    - **Transactions** — acquisitions, mergers, and strategic investments

    This breadth means you can build highly specific search queries — for example, finding B2B SaaS companies in Southeast Asia with $2M–$10M estimated ARR, fewer than 50 employees, and recent interest from Tier 1 VCs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does Specter source its data?">
    Specter aggregates data from a combination of public, private, and proprietary sources, then applies AI processing and human verification to ensure accuracy:

    - **60,000+ media outlets** — news articles, press releases, and trade publications
    - **Social media** — X/Twitter, LinkedIn, and other professional networks
    - **Financial filings and regulatory databases** — disclosed funding, revenue, and corporate structure data
    - **Podcasts and video content** — AI-transcribed interviews and founder discussions
    - **Proprietary signals** — Specter's own network and data partnerships

    Every data point passes through Specter's AI verification pipeline, with human review applied to high-impact or ambiguous signals. This layered approach gives you data you can act on with confidence.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I find stealth companies on Specter?">
    Yes. Specter is one of the few platforms that surfaces stealth and pre-public companies through talent signals. When a founder, serial entrepreneur, or senior executive leaves a high-growth company or unicorn and goes dark on LinkedIn, Specter's signal engine picks up on those movements and flags them as potential stealth activity.

    This means you can identify promising founders before they announce their next company — often months before they appear on any other platform.

    <Tip>
      Use Specter's talent signal filters to track departures from specific companies (e.g., ex-Stripe, ex-Revolut) or role types (e.g., CTO or Head of Product) to build a pipeline of pre-stealth founders.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How accurate is the revenue data?">
    Specter's revenue estimates are generated using AI models trained on thousands of verified revenue disclosures, then anchored to any publicly available or disclosed figures for each company. Each estimate includes a confidence band so you can calibrate how much weight to place on it.

    Key things to know:

    - Estimates are updated continuously as new signals arrive (e.g., a founder mentions ARR in a podcast, or a press release cites growth figures)
    - Human reviewers verify high-confidence signals before they are published
    - Where verified disclosures exist, the model anchors tightly to those figures

    <Note>
      Revenue estimates are models, not audited financials. Use them as a directional signal for prioritization and market mapping, and corroborate with primary diligence for investment decisions.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## API & Integrations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I get an API key?">
    Once you have a Specter subscription, you can generate an API key directly from your account dashboard:

    1. Log in at [app.tryspecter.com](https://app.tryspecter.com)
    2. Navigate to **Settings → API**
    3. Click **Generate API Key** and copy your key

    Use your key by passing it in the `X-API-Key` header with every request to the Specter API base URL: `https://app.tryspecter.com/api/v1`.

    <Note>
      Keep your API key secure. Do not expose it in client-side code or public repositories. If your key is compromised, you can revoke and regenerate it from the same settings page.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I'm getting a 401 error on the API — what should I do?">
    A `401 Unauthorized` error means the API could not authenticate your request. Here's how to resolve it:

    1. **Check the header name** — Make sure you're using `X-API-Key` (not `Authorization` or `Bearer`). Example:
       ```http
       GET https://app.tryspecter.com/api/v1/companies
       X-API-Key: your_api_key_here
       ```
    2. **Confirm the key is active** — Log in to [app.tryspecter.com](https://app.tryspecter.com), go to **Settings → API**, and verify your key is listed and not revoked.
    3. **Check for whitespace** — Copy-paste errors can introduce invisible spaces before or after the key. Make sure the value is trimmed.
    4. **Confirm your subscription is active** — API access requires an active Specter subscription. If your account has lapsed, contact [azher@tryspecter.com](mailto:azher@tryspecter.com).

    If you've checked all of the above and are still seeing 401 errors, reach out to [azher@tryspecter.com](mailto:azher@tryspecter.com) with your API key prefix (first 6 characters) and we'll investigate.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My data looks outdated — how do I refresh it?">
    Specter's data refreshes automatically in the background — there is no manual "sync" button required. If you're seeing data that looks stale, here are a few things to check:

    - **Hard refresh your browser** — Press `Ctrl+Shift+R` (Windows/Linux) or `Cmd+Shift+R` (Mac) to bypass any cached views in the web app.
    - **Check the data timestamp** — Each company profile shows a "last updated" indicator. If it's more than a few days old for a high-activity company, that's worth flagging.
    - **Use the "Flag Data" option** — In any company profile, you can flag specific fields as potentially outdated. Our team reviews flagged entries on a rolling basis.
    - **Contact support** — If the issue persists or affects multiple records, email [azher@tryspecter.com](mailto:azher@tryspecter.com) with the company name(s) and the field(s) in question.

    <Note>
      Some data points — particularly for very early-stage or stealth companies — may update less frequently due to limited public signal availability. This is expected behavior.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
